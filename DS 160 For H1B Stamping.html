DS 160 Instructions GUIDE

This guide helps to provide on instructions in completing the DS 160 for a person who is working in USA on H1B and requires Stamping in India when he vists india during his return.

1.DS-160 APPLICATION INSTRUCTIONS

Ensure your DS-160 reflects your current H-1B status and your full immigration history (F-1 to H-1B COS).

A. Purpose of Trip (The Core Application)

Field Required Selection/Entry
**Purpose of Trip to U.S.** **TEMPORARY WORKER (H)**
**Specify** **H1B**
**Application Receipt/Petition Number** Your 13-character **USCIS Receipt Number** from your I-797 (e.g., SRC2512345678). Omit dashes.

B. Previous U.S. Travel History (F-1 to H-1B)

Question Your Answer Explanation
**Have you ever been in the U.S.?** **YES** You are currently present in the U.S.
**Provide information on your last five U.S. visits:** The exact Date of Entry from your most recent entry stamp in your passport or your electronic I-94 record. This is the date you last crossed the border (usually an airport) into the U.S.
**Do you or did you ever hold a U.S. Driverâ€™s License?** **YES** Driver's License Number and From which State your Driver's License is issued
**Have you ever been issued a U.S. Visa?** **YES** You had an F-1 visa stamp for initial entry.
**Date Last Visa Was Issued** The Issue Date. This is usually listed clearly on the stamp, often next to the field labeled "Issue Date" or "Date Issued." Typically found near the bottom right of the visa stamp.
**Visa Number** The Visa Foil Number. This is a unique number used to identify your visa document. It is a distinctive red number (usually 8 characters, sometimes beginning with a letter) printed on the bottom right side of the visa stamp
**Are you applying for the same type of visa?** **NO** You were issued F-1; you are applying for H-1B. (F-1 != H-1B).
**Have you been ten-printed?** **YES** You had full fingerprinting for your F-1 visa interview.
